Katakana カタカナ

Five lessons take you from zero katakana to reading every character — all 46 base shapes, 25 voiced (dakuten/handakuten) variants, 33 yōon combinations, plus the long-vowel mark ー and the small ッ consonant doubler. Katakana spells the same sounds as hiragana — the audio is reused, since カ and か are both pronounced "ka." What's new is the shapes (angular, not curvy) and the when (foreign loanwords, proper nouns, sound effects, emphasis).

Pack 2 builds directly on Pack 1. Every row, every dakuten rule, every yōon pattern works the same way. The main lift is just visually relearning the shapes. Lesson 5 then teaches the two katakana-specific marks (ー and ッ) plus when katakana is the right script to use.

Two famous traps: シ vs ツ (look at the slash direction — シ slashes up-and-to-the-right, ツ has three downward drops) and ソ vs ン (ソ leans right with two strokes; ン leans left with the strokes meeting at the bottom). Lessons 2 and 3 call these out where they appear.
